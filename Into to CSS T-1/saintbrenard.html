SaintBernard

Description:

The St. Bernard or Saint Bernard is a breed of very large working dog from the Western Alps in Italy and Switzerland. They were originally bred for rescue work by the hospice of the Great St Bernard Pass on the Italian-Swiss border. The hospice, built by and named after the Alpine monk Saint Bernard of Menthon, acquired its first dogs between 1660 and 1670.The breed has become famous through tales of Alpine rescues, as well as for its large size and gentle temperament.

Characteristics:

Calm and easygoing, the Saint Bernard is a low-energy dog and a slow mover. It learns quickly but tends to respond methodically. The Saint is very affectionate and demonstrative toward family and fairly friendly toward strangers.

Appearance:

Size: Males are 70 to 75 cm tall and weigh 63 to 81 kg, while females are 65 to 70 cm tall and weigh 54 to 63 kg.

Coat: Saint Bernards can have short or long hair, and their coats can be red and white, brown and white, or brindle and white. Rough-coated dogs have longer hair and feathering on their legs and thighs.

Head: Saint Bernards have a massive head with a short, wide muzzle, black lips and nose, and a wrinkled forehead.

Eyes: Saint Bernards have medium-sized, dark brown eyes that are set deep and slightly droopy.

Ears: Saint Bernards have floppy ears that are set high on their head.

Tail: Saint Bernards have a long, heavy tail that hangs high when they are relaxed.

Legs: Saint Bernards have muscular legs with large feet and well-arched toes.

Jowls: Saint Bernards have jowls, which makes them prone to drooling.
